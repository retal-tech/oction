Oticon More™ research

In order to deliver what the brain needs to make sense of sound, Oticon builds hearing aids on the philosophy that better hearing starts with the brain. This is BrainHearing™. Oticon More continues to build upon the proven benefits BrainHearing provides through use of our life-changing technology.



More evidence shows a leap in performance

We used an advanced version of our pupillometry testing to evaluate the real-world effectiveness of Oticon More. To determine listening effort, changes in pupil size were recorded while participants were given complex tasks to solve while following conversations and replying to them over a sustained time frame.

Findings revealed MoreSound Intelligence™ (MSI) in Oticon More reduced sustained listening effort in realistic situations by an astounding 30%.* This means when MSI is turned on, your patients get more sound to the brain with less cognitive effort required to listen to speech in noise.


*Murmu Nielsen, R. & Ng, E. 2021. Reducing sustained listening effort: Oticon More whitepaper.

Better sound quality

Participants were asked to rate the sound quality of each of the hearing aids in several real-life sound environments. Oticon More was rated significantly higher than its competitors. In fact, up to 8 out of 10 people preferred Oticon More over its competitors when listening to conversations in scenes such as cafes, restaurants, and speech with a facemask.*


*Man BKL, Garnæs MF, Løve S. Oticon More competitive benchmark Part 2-Clinical Evidence. 2021.
Santurette S, Lu X, Emert CA, Man BKL. Oticon More competitor benchmark Part-1. Technical Evidence. 2021.

Read the Clinical Evidence

More speech

For excellent speech understanding, it is vital that the brain receives as much speech information as possible — especially in challenging listening environments. Therefore, we compared how much speech information each hearing aid provides in a noisy cafe environment. Talkers were positioned toward the front of the listener at a -15° angle, and toward the side of the listener at a -60° angle. Results show that Oticon More gives patients up to 20% more speech information than competitors in both scenarios.*


*Man BKL, Garnæs MF, Løve S. Oticon More competitive benchmark Part 2-Clinical Evidence. 2021.
Santurette S, Lu X, Emert CA, Man BKL. Oticon More competitor benchmark Part-1. Technical Evidence. 2021.

Read the Technical Evidence

Faster adaptation

The ability to adapt quickly to changes in the sound scene is vital so that patients do not miss important speech information. We tested how quickly Oticon More and the two competitors adapt to changing environments. The results show Oticon More adapts 2-3 times faster than the competition.*


*Man BKL, Garnæs MF, Løve S. Oticon More competitive benchmark Part 2-Clinical Evidence. 2021.
Santurette S, Lu X, Emert CA, Man BKL. Oticon More competitor benchmark Part-1. Technical Evidence. 2021.

More and clearer sounds to the brain

New independent research has shown that the brain needs access to all sounds — not just speech — in order to work in a natural way. And now, new and innovative research methods prove how Oticon More delivers just that.


From testing of brain activity, using EEG, the strength of the EEG signal shows MoreSound Intelligence, in Oticon More, makes the full sound scene 60% clearer.** This ability to represent all relevant sounds in the brain is crucial for a patient’s ability to navigate in environments that are dynamic and complex.


When compared to our best hearing aid until now, Oticon Opn S™, we see that Oticon More delivers 30% more sound to the brain.* For patients, this means getting access to the full and precisely balanced sound scene.


Better speech understanding with less effort

Because Oticon More gives the brain access to more sound, it makes it easier for the brain to understand speech. Tests show Oticon More increases speech understanding by 15% when compared to Oticon Opn S.*

At the same time, Oticon More is also proven to reduce listening effort, enabling people to remember even more of what’s being said.*

*Santurette, et al. 2020. Oticon More clinical evidence. Oticon Whitepaper.
**EEG testing with MoreSound Intelligence in on vs. off setting, from Santurette et al.

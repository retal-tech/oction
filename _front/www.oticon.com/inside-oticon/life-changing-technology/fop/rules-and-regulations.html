2022 Focus on People Awards

Official rules

NO PURCHASE NECESSARY TO ENTER OR WIN. A PURCHASE WILL NOT IMPROVE YOUR CHANCES OF WINNING. 

  1. Promotion Period/Sponsor: Oticon Focus on People Awards is conducted in two phases. Phase 1 “Call for Nominations” begins on 6/13/22 and ends on 7/22/22 (the “Nominations Period”). Phase 2 begins on 9/26/22 and ends on 11/4/22 (the “Voting Period”). Note that phase dates may be subject to change. Sponsor: Oticon, Inc. 580 Howard Avenue, Somerset, NJ 08873. Awards program is governed by US law. Void where prohibited.

  2. Eligibility: The Awards program is open to individuals with any degree of hearing loss who are: legal residents of the 50 United States or District of Columbia, age 6 or older. Nominees under the age of 18 years require the written approval of parent or legal guardian. Nominees in the Practitioner category are the exception — individuals nominated in this category are not required to have a hearing loss. Employees of Sponsor, its affiliated companies and its advertising and promotion agencies and anyone involved with the Awards program (collectively, “Awards Entities”), and their immediate family members and/or those living in the same household of each are not eligible to enter or win.

  3. How to enter: To be eligible for the Awards program, all completed nomination forms must be received during the Nomination Period. To enter go to the www.oticon.com/FOP ("Awards Website") and complete and submit the nomination form. There is no limit to the number of nominations each person (Nominator) may submit.

    Nominations may be submitted on the awards website: www.oticon.com/FOP 

  4. Selection of Finalists: Nominations will be reviewed by a team comprised of Oticon representatives and an external consultant in July & August. Each nominee is evaluated based on achievements/contributions to helping to change the perceptions of hearing loss and their ability to serve as a role model for all but especially for people with hearing loss. Finalists will be notified of their selection by email/phone.

    Three finalists will be selected in each of four categories:
    • Student, for full-time students with hearing loss, ages 6 – 25;
    • Adult, for people with hearing loss, ages 21 and above; 
    • Advocacy, for volunteers with hearing loss, who are actively involved in support efforts for the hard-of-hearing and deaf community 
    • Practitioner category recognizes hearing care professionals who go “above and beyond” to change the world for people with hearing loss through humanitarian and public education efforts. Nominees in the Practitioner category are not required to have a hearing loss to qualify.

    Upon notification of their selection, finalists are required to complete release forms authorizing Oticon to feature them in the online voting phase and provide their social security number for 1099 filing. Children under age 18 require signed permission from parent or legal guardian. All finalists must provide photos of themselves. They also must sign release forms and approve copy used for the online voting.

    As part of the release, if a finalist is selected as first place winner and cannot participate in the Awards ceremony (see requirement re: ceremony participation in Section 7), Oticon will select another finalist from their category as first place winner. Materials describing the finalists are approved by finalists prior to posting on the voting website.

  5. Voting: The voting pages on Oticon.com will officially open on 9/26/22. One-time voting session (with one vote for each of the four finalists for a total of four votes) per email address is allowed. Finalists are provided with press announcement materials for use on their websites, social media pages and with local media in their communities. Oticon will publicize the call for votes through professional and general news media and online advertising.

    Voting ends on 11/4/22. Oticon will notify first, second and third place winners no later than 1/31/23.

    Official announcement of the winners will be communicated on Oticon.com and in media and will coincide with the timing of the Awards ceremony.

  6. Prizes: First place winners receive a $1,000 cash prize and a $1,000 donation to the charity of their choice. First place winners in the Student, Adult and Advocacy categories also receive Oticon BrainHearing™ hearing devices (estimated value $6,000). Hearing aids must be claimed no later than 6 months from the program close.

    Oticon provides a $1,000 donation to a designated charity for each first place winner. Winners select a charity of their choice for the donation.

    Second place winners receive a $500 cash prize and third place winners, a $250 cash prize.

  7. Awards Ceremony: Awards will be officially presented to the four first place winners at a special Oticon Focus on People Awards Ceremony. If the ceremony is scheduled as an in-person event, winners agree to attend in person to accept their award. Oticon, Inc. will cover cost of travel to/from the Awards ceremony for first place winners only and ground transportation to/from airport at the event location. Hotel and dining costs are also covered for first place winners. Only one hotel room is provided for each winner.

    For the student first place winner, Oticon will also cover the airfare for parent/guardian to travel with the winner. Winner in the student category, under age 18, must be accompanied by a parent/guardian.

    If the Awards ceremony is conducted as a virtual event, each first place award winner agrees to record and submit a short acceptance video for display during the virtual event. The instructions to record and submit videos and video content specifications for each award type will be provided by Oticon, Inc.
